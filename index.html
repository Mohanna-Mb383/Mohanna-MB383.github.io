Research Focus

Graph Neural Networks for Bacterial Phylogenetics

My research combines graph neural networks (GNNs), transformer architectures, and topological data analysis to address phylogenetic inference problems in large bacterial datasets.

Current directions
  • Sequence encodings based on cgMLST, SNP, and gap-aware representations.
  • Axial-attention and graph-based architectures for scalable tree and network inference.
  • Robust models for missing data and complex evolutionary scenarios.
Methods and ideas
  • GNN-based architectures tailored to phylogenetic graphs.
  • Transformer-style encoders for bacterial sequence collections.
  • Generative approaches inspired by models such as PHYLOVAE.
